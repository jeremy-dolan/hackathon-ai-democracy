Participant information and Consent

This survey is informing an explorative hackathon project. Before you decide, you need to understand why the project is being done and what it would involve for you. Please take the time to read the following information carefully. Talk to others about the survey if you wish.

Please ask us if there is anything that is not clear or if you would like more information. Take time to decide whether or not you wish to take part.

Who is organising and funding the project?
The project is part of a hackathon and does not receive external funding.

What is the project about?
The project is about original and AI generated comments on a student debt relief policy.

What would taking part involve?
You will read comments and decide how likely you think they are AI generated. Moreover, you will be asked if the comments affect your likelihood to support the policy.

Do I have to take part?
No. Participation in this survey is completely voluntary and choosing not to take part will not affect you in any way. You can also choose to withdraw your participation without giving a reason. Further details about withdrawing from the study are provided later on in this document.

What are the possible benefits of taking part in this survey?
You can get a feeling for current AI capabilities and comments on US policies in general.

What are the possible disadvantages, side effects or risks, of taking part in this survey?
There are no known disadvantages beyond the time you spend on the survey.

Expenses and payments
There are no payments for participation in the survey.

Will my taking part be kept confidential?
Yes, we collect no personal identifiable data. Only the project team has access to the data.

What will happen to the data collected about me?
No identifiable data will be collected from you as part of this survey. This means that once your responses have been submitted to the project team, it will not be possible to withdraw this data as your individual responses cannot be identified.

What will happen to the results of the survey?
The results of the survey will be used in aggregate form for a hackathon report. This report will be visible on www.apartresearch.com

Who should I contact if I want further information?
maihackathon24@gmail.com

Introduction

 
 

You are a policymaker. In that role, you have to evaluate public comments about a proposed rule for student loan debt relief. Some of the comments may be AI-generated. Read each comment, and assess (1) whether you think the comment was written by a human or generated by an AI, and (2) to what degree you would take the content of the comment into account when deciding whether to support this policy.

You will evaluate comments regarding the following policy:
The policy addresses the sharp increase in the cost of four-year postsecondary education, which has outpaced the coverage of Pell Grants and significantly increased reliance on Federal student loans, leading to a cumulative debt of $1.6 trillion among over 43 million borrowers. In response, the Department of Education proposes regulations to provide targeted debt relief by specifying the Secretary's authority to waive portions of Federal student loan debt under various conditions, aiming to alleviate the financial burden on borrowers, especially those from vulnerable low-income backgrounds.
